### Clinical Mechanism
The interaction between NUDT15 and thiopurine has significant clinical implications. NUDT15 is primarily responsible for the metabolism of thiopurine. 

### Clinical Consequence
Poor metabolizers of NUDT15 experience significantly altered systemic exposure to thiopurine, leading to increased risk of toxicity or lack of efficacy. Normal metabolizers achieve standard therapeutic concentrations.

### CPIC Dosing Recommendation
For poor metabolizers, consider an alternative medication or a drastic dose reduction (e.g., 50%). For rapid/ultrarapid metabolizers, standard dosing may be inadequate, requiring close monitoring or alternative therapy.
